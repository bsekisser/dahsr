<h3 align="center"><font size="5" face="Georgia"><strong><u>Jane
Scott</u></strong></font></h3>

<p><font face="Georgia">Jane Scott, daughter of Daniel Scott,
Sr., and Jane Johnson was born about 1686 in Baltimore County.
She married Francis Watkins and they had a son named Francis. Her
husband, Francis Watkins Sr., died in 1713 and on November 14,
1714 she married Samuel Hughes, son of Matthias Hughes. On June
19, 1736 Samuel and Jane sold King's Increase (100 acres) to
Gregory Farmer. In 1737 they are listed as living in Spesutia
Lower Hundred. On July 28, 1739 they bought King's Increase back
from Gregory Farmer. In April, 1746 the vestry of St. John's
Parish summoned Samuel Hughes to appear before them on charges of
unlawful cohabitation with Hannah Litton, alias Jones. In the
1750 debt book Jane Hughes is listed as owning a 26 acre part of
Hathaway's Hazard. In 1757 Jane reported her husband to the
vestry of St. John's parish for unlawful cohabitation with
Henrietta Jones. </font></p>

<p><font face="Georgia">Jane Scott Watkins Hughes' will was
written on April 14, 1762 and proved on October 31, 1765. She
named her children; daughters Margaret Hanson, Sarah Little, and
Jane Osborn, and her grandchildren; Thomas Hughes of son John,
Thomas Little and John Hall Hughes of daughter Sarah; Daniel,
Francis, Jane, Elizabeth, Nathaniel, and Daniel Scott Watkins,
children of son Francis Watkins.</font></p>

<p><font face="Georgia">Jane Scott Watkins Hughes and Samuel
Hughes had the following children: Margaret b. October 18, 1716;
John, born March 15, 1719; Jane, born May 30, 1721; Sarah, born
October 15, 1723; Mary, born July 4, 1725.</font></p>
