<h3 align="center"><font face="Georgia"><strong><u>Will of
Matthias Hughes</u></strong></font></h3>

<p align="left"><font face="Georgia">In the name of God, Amen,
the 21st day of January, 1702, I, Matthias Hughes, of the Parish
of St. James in the County of Anne Arundel, planter, being sick
and weak in body but of perfect mind and memory thanks be given
to God and therefor and calling to mind the mortality of my God
knowing that it is appointed for account now and to pay do make
and ordain this my last will, that is to say:<br>
FIRST:I give and recommend my soul unto the hands of God that
gave it and for my body I commend it to the earth to be buried in
a like and decent manner under the direction of my executor
nothing doubting but at the great resurrection. I take reward the
same again by the mighty power of God and at leaving such worldly
estate whatsoever it hath pleased God to possess me with in this
life I give and dispose of my land in manner and form following
to wit:</font></p>

<p align="left"><font face="Georgia">I give and bequeath unto my
dearly beloved sons, Samuel and John all that tract of land
called Papa Ridge to be equally divided between them, that is to
say I give unto my son Samuel Hughes, his heirs and assigns
forever that half part on which Mr. Harsewood resideth. I give
and bequeath unto my son, John Hughes, his heirs and assigns
forever the other half of the said land.</font></p>

<p align="left"><font face="Georgia">I give unto my son Samuel
one young calf of my young cow named Cherry with all her female
increase.</font></p>

<p align="left"><font face="Georgia">I give and bequeath unto my
son John one mare and one cow named Catch with all her female
increase and one hand mill.</font></p>

<p align="left"><font face="Georgia">Is my will and desire that
the four hogsheads of tobacco when sold from Mr. Harsewood for
the rent of my land be equally divided between my two sons Samuel
and John aforesaid.</font></p>

<p align="left"><font face="Georgia">I give unto Sarah Little one
feather bed of furniture thereunto belonging. I give and bequeath
my son John unto the said Sarah Little to be carefully brought up
by her until he shall arrive to the age of nineteen years. Which
so said Sarah Little I like, write, constitute, make and ordain
my only and last executor of this my last will and testament.</font></p>

<p align="left"><font face="Georgia">I give unto my daughters
Sarah and Margaret, 12 pieces of sterling a piece and I do hereby
utterly disallow, revoke and disannul all and every former
testament and wills, legacies and bequests, executed by me in any
way before this time named wills and requiring, ratifying and
confirming this and no other to be my last will and testament. In
witness whereof I have hereunto set my hand and seal this day and
year above written signed and sealed published pronounced and
declared by the said Matthias Hughes as his last will and
testament in the presence of the subscribers, viz.;</font></p>

<p align="right"><font face="Georgia">his<br>
Matthias 4 Hughes<br>
mark<br>
Thomas Trott<br>
(T) his mark W. E. Beecher<br>
March 22, 1702<br>
Alexander Cadal<br>
John Beecher</font></p>

<p align="left"><font face="Georgia">These Prove in common before
me.<br>
Abra. Burkhead<br>
From:<br>
Libre TB 11, Folio 349 &amp; 350<br>
Hall of Records, Annapolis, Md.</font></p>

<p align="center">Testamentary Proceedings<br>
26 April 1703<br>
</p>

<p align="left">Then came Sarah Little Executrix of ye said part
of will and testament of Matthias Hughes and prayed commission to
Mr. Abraham Berkhead to take ye probate of ye said will and that
she be granted letters of administration she was thereby ordered
accordingly and commissioned immediately with bond.</p>

<p align="left">Maryland State Records, Annapolis Md., Libre 19A,
Folio 165.</p>
